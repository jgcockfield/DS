The Site

Before the pyramids of Egypt were complete, a civilization arose in the lower Mississippi Valley and built something extraordinary. With no metal tools, no draft animals, and no agriculture to speak of, they moved over 53 million cubic feet of earth by hand — creating a complex of concentric ridges, plazas, and mounds that stretches nearly a mile across.

"Poverty Point challenges everything we thought we knew about what hunter-gatherers could build — and why."

For decades, archaeologists assumed it was a trade hub. Then LiDAR stripped away the trees and revealed the true scale. Now the questions have only deepened: Who were these people? What drew thousands of them here? And why did they stop?

This is a conversation about what we know, what we've inferred — and what remains one of the great mysteries of North American archaeology.

Ground Truth
USGS 3DEP LiDAR · QL1 0.5m Resolution

LiDAR hillshade revealing the concentric ridge system, Mound A, and the full extent of the earthworks — invisible to the naked eye beneath centuries of vegetation. This is the geometric ground truth everything else is built on.
